What is GPS Tracking?

GPS tracking is the surveillance of location through the use of the GPS (Global Positioning System) to track the location of an object like Bus, Train, Car remotely. The technology pinpoint longitude, latitude, ground speed, and course direction of the target.

The Global Positioning System is a "constellation" of approximately 30 well-spaced satellites that move in earth orbit and make it possible for people and companies with ground receivers to pinpoint their geographic location.

GPS tracking is precious for IT Company, Police, Firefighters, Military and large carrier businesses. many companies use Automatic Vehicle Locator systems. AVL systems involve tracks of vehicles that are each implemented with a mobile radio receiver.

How does GPS Tracking Software work?

There is 24 satellite included GPS in the space, they always spin around the earth 365 days in a year and 24 hours in a day and they complete one round in 12 hours.GPS (Global Positioning System) is a type of GNSS ( Global Navigation Satellite System). It Provides tiny electronic receivers to define the latitude, longitude, and altitude of a point.

Whenever your device gets a signal from one satellite (for example Red) then you should be somewhere at the red sphere which center is the satellite and radius is the distance between your device and satellite.

Now when your device gets signal from the second satellite (let’s assume yellow)then you should be on the Red sphere and also on the yellow sphere means you are on the intersecting circle of two spheres. Now you are in the circle.

Now when your device gets signal from the third satellite(blue) then you get the exact position of your device, the intersecting point of three spheres.
This how the user is using GPS Tracking Software in Device.

GPS manages a network of satellites that are orbiting the Earth. The time you turn your GPS, the receiver receives the signal from these satellites about their current time and location. The receiver then estimates its distance from the satellite based on the event taken by the signal to reach you.
